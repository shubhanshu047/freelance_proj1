Know about the Symptoms

What is PSORIASIS ?

Psoriasis is a stubborn, long-term (chronic) skin problem that causes skin cells to grow too quickly, resulting in thick, white, silvery, or red patches of skin.

Symptoms of PSORIASIS include:

- Raised, bright red patches of skin, covered with loose, silvery scales.

- Small areas of bleeding where the skin scales are scraped.

- Scales, crusted plaques on the skin with itching and sudden flare-ups.

- Develop after injuries like cuts, burn, or too much exposure to the sun.

- Patches may develop on joints along with joint swelling, tenderness, and pain.


What causes PSORIASIS ?

Psychological factors that we have observed in our patients with Psoriasis are:


- Chronic, unexpressed *grief*

- Extreme anxiety to cope with separation and merger situations

- Inability to express emotions verbally

- Extreme levels of stress.

- These are found in varying degrees in different patients.

What is a Heel Spur?

A heel spur is a bony outgrowth that forms on the underside or back of the heel bone (calcaneus). It’s your body’s way of responding to chronic stress or inflammation

Symptoms of Heel Spur include:

- Sharp, stabbing pain in the heel—especially with first steps in the morning.

- Dull ache after long periods of standing or walking.

- Tenderness or swelling around the heel.

- Pain that worsens with activity but eases with rest.

- Visible lump or bump (in some cases).

- Stiffness in the foot or ankle.


What causes Heel Spur?

- Repetitive strain from running, jumping, or standing long hours.

- Improper footwear (tight shoes, high heels, or flat soles).

- Flat feet or high arches that alter foot mechanics.

- Obesity or excess weight adding pressure to the heel.

- Aging, which reduces the heel’s natural cushioning.

- Inflammatory conditions like arthritis.

- Poor posture or gait disorders.

Recurrent Cold & Cough?

Recurrent cold and cough refers to frequent episodes of nasal congestion, sneezing, sore throat, and chest irritation that return again and again

Symptoms of Recurrent Cold & Cough include:

- Runny or blocked nose.

- Frequent sneezing or throat irritation.

- Dry or wet cough that lingers for days.

- Fatigue or low energy during episodes.

- Headache or heaviness in the sinuses.


What causes Recurrent Cold & Cough?

These symptoms often arise from a mix of physical, emotional, and environmental triggers:


- Low immunity due to poor nutrition or chronic stress.

- Allergies to dust, pollen, cold drinks, or weather changes.

- Gut imbalance affecting immune response.

- Exposure to viruses or pollutants.

- Overuse of antibiotics, weakening natural defenses.

Asthma?

Asthma is a chronic condition where the airways in the lungs become narrowed, inflamed, and hypersensitive, making breathing difficult

Symptoms of Asthma include:

- Wheezing (whistling sound while breathing)

- Shortness of breath, especially during exertion or at night.

- Dry or persistent cough

- Chest tightness or heaviness

- Difficulty sleeping due to breathlessness


What causes Asthma?

Asthma is multi-factorial, often triggered by a mix of physical, emotional, and environmental factors:


- Genetic predisposition or family history

- Allergies to dust, pollen, smoke, chemicals, or food

- Emotional stress—grief, anxiety, or suppressed emotions

- Climatic changes—cold air, humidity, or monsoon season

- Respiratory infections or frequent colds

Back Pain?

Back pain is the body’s way of whispering or sometimes shouting that something is out of alignment. It can arise from the muscles, bones, nerves, or discs in the spine, and may be acute (short-term) or chronic (long-term).

Symptoms of Back Pain include:

- Dull ache or sharp stabbing pain in the back

- Stiffness, especially after rest or in the morning

- Numbness or tingling in arms, hands, or legs

- Muscle weakness or spasms

- Headaches (especially in cervical cases)

- Reduced flexibility or range of motion


What causes Back Pain?

- Aging & Wear-and-Tear: Discs lose hydration and elasticity

- Nutritional Deficiencies: Especially calcium, magnesium, and Vitamin D

- Poor Posture: Long hours at desks or screens

- Repetitive Strain: Lifting, bending, or twisting

- Injuries or Trauma: Past accidents or falls

- Genetic Predisposition: Family history of spinal issues
